## Variance Analysis

### Line 2: Community Educator Training (Underspend: $33,000 / 22.3%)

Nairobi field office reports that formal educator training sessions were difficult to fill during Q1-Q2. The field team redirected training-related activities to informal community-based workshops that were not budgeted under this line. The underspend correlates with the low dashboard count (31 of 80 target educators trained formally). The field director reports 47 additional informal workshops that were not logged in the HQ system.

**Compliance note:** The 22.3% underspend exceeds the 10% variance reporting threshold in Section 6.3 and requires written justification in the mid-term review documentation.

### Line 3: Community Mobilization (Overspend: $37,000 / 39.4%)

The Nairobi field office reports that funds were redirected from the training budget to mobilization activities (community events, school visits, parent meetings) to address an enrollment shortfall in Q3 of Year 1. The field director reports this redirection drove a 35% enrollment improvement in Q4.

**Compliance note:** Budget Line 3 (Community Mobilization) exceeds the 15% per-line flexibility clause in Grant Agreement Section 6.1 by 24.4 percentage points. The Section 6.1 threshold is 15% without prior written approval; the actual variance is 39.4%. Documentation of prior written approval has not been located. A formal waiver application is required before final grant reconciliation.

## Summary

The overall Nairobi program is **$13,000 under total budget** (-1.4%), indicating sound overall financial management. The compliance issue is concentrated in the **per-line variance on Community Mobilization (Line 3)**, which at 39.4% significantly exceeds the 15% flexibility clause. This requires a formal waiver application as specified in Section 6.1 of the grant agreement.
