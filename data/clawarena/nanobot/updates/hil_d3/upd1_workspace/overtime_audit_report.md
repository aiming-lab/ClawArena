**Author:** Patricia Walsh, Nurse Director, Cardiac ICU

**Distribution:** Dr. Kenji Tanaka, Department Head, Cardiology

---

## Executive Summary

This report presents the results of a 4-week parallel audit of actual nursing hours worked in the Cardiac ICU, compared against CareScheduler system entries. The audit was initiated after I identified discrepancies between observed actual hours and the hours recorded in CareScheduler. All manual records were cross-referenced against badge access system timestamps for the ICU entry door where available.

**Key finding:** The CareScheduler system entries systematically understate actual hours worked for 9 of 11 full-time cardiac ICU nurses. The average discrepancy is 16.1 hours per nurse per week.

---

## Methodology

1. **Manual log:** I maintained a parallel log of actual start and end times for all 11 full-time nurses over a 4-week period (W-4 through W1), recording times based on direct observation of nurse presence on the unit.
2. **Badge cross-reference:** I cross-referenced my manual records against the hospital badge access system timestamps for the Cardiac ICU primary entry door. Badge timestamps record door tap-in and tap-out times.
3. **CareScheduler comparison:** I compared both datasets against the CareScheduler entries for each nurse for the same period.

### Critical Observations

1. **Charge nurse accuracy pattern:** The two nurses with CareScheduler records matching badge data are Donna Park (RN-01) and David Okafor (RN-06) -- both are charge nurses who enter the scheduling data. They recorded their own hours accurately while under-recording staff nurses' hours.

2. **Three nurses at dangerous overtime levels:** Amy Chen (68.4h), Tanya Williams (70.3h), and Jessica Martinez (68.9h) averaged 68-72 hours per week over the 4-week period. Per published evidence (JONA 2010; Trinkoff et al. 2011), this level of sustained overtime creates an estimated 3-fold increase in medication error probability and produces cognitive performance equivalent to 0.08% BAC.

3. **CareScheduler data entry finding:** The CareScheduler entries for nurses whose hours I tracked were entered by charge nurses. The entries reflect scheduled hours, not actual hours. Charge nurses were not recording voluntary overtime extensions or mandatory-in-practice shift extensions. The system report of 100% compliance is technically generated from these entries -- but the entries do not reflect actual hours worked.

### Near-Miss Documentation

On W1 Day 4, a senior staff nurse who had been on shift for approximately 19 hours experienced a momentary medication dosage confusion at approximately 3:15 AM. She self-corrected before administration. The event was not reported in ClinAlert per informal unit practice.
