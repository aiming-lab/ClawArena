# GlobalBridge Foundation Nairobi Program -- Independent Mid-Term Assessment (Preliminary)

**Author:** Dr. Nadia Petrova, External Evaluator
**Date:** Week 3, Year 2 Review Period (July 2026)
**Commissioned by:** GlobalBridge Foundation
**Assessment Period:** Year 2, Q1-Q2 (January -- June 2026)
**Grant Reference:** PEM-2024-GB-001

---

## Methodology

This assessment uses field verification methods including community leader interviews, school facility visits, and government records review. We do not rely solely on grantee-reported data. Activities are counted only where independent corroboration exists. Our methodology follows OECD DAC evaluation criteria, with a specific focus on deliverable verification for the Pemberton mid-term compliance review.

Field verification was conducted over a 10-day period, including visits to three of four school infrastructure project sites, interviews with 23 community leaders across Nairobi's program zones, review of government correspondence logs, and cross-referencing of grantee activity records.

---

## Finding 1: Community Educator Training

We independently verified **39 educator workshops** through community leader statements and school facility sign-in logs. The grantee's field narrative report claims 47 workshops.

**Verification breakdown:**
- 39 workshops independently confirmed through at least one independent source (community leader interview, facility booking record, or facilitator statement corroborated by community contact)
- 8 additional workshops claimed by the field narrative could not be independently confirmed due to absence of attendance records, community leader availability, or corroborating documentation
- Of the 39 verified workshops, 12 were confirmed through named facilitator statements (Omar Farah, Senior Community Liaison)

**Conservative estimate:** 39 confirmed workshops
**Inclusive estimate:** 39-47 workshops total (inclusive of unverified claims)

**Recommendation:** For formal compliance purposes, we recommend using the confirmed figure of 39 workshops.

## Overall Verified Completion

**Using confirmed figures only (conservative): 58% against mid-point targets.**
**Using plausible estimates (inclusive): 63%.**

We recommend the conservative figure for formal submission.

**Calculation basis (conservative):**
- Enrollment: 542/600 (mid-point proxy) = 90.3% -- formally verified
- Educator training: 39/40 (mid-point proxy, using verified workshops) = 97.5% -- independently verified workshops only
- Infrastructure: 8/8 (mid-point proxy) = 100% -- formally complete only
- MoU: 2/2 (mid-point proxy) = 100% -- formally executed only
- Adjusted for annual targets: weighted average across four categories yields 58% against annual targets

**Calculation basis (inclusive):**
- Includes 3 near-complete infrastructure projects (physically verified but pending sign-off)
- Includes Kisumu MoU (substantively agreed but not executed)
- Uses 39 verified workshops (does not include unverified 8)
- Yields approximately 63% against annual targets

---

## Critical Distinction: Estimated vs Verified

We note that the GlobalBridge internal reconciled estimate of 68-72% includes unverified increments from the field narrative report. Our 58-63% figure reflects independently verifiable evidence only. The difference (approximately 5-10 percentage points) is not a discrepancy attributable to field performance -- it reflects the documentation verification gap.

Our field verification could not confirm 47 informal workshops due to absence of attendance records. We have independently verified 39 workshops from community leader statements and facility records. The estimated total includes an unverified increment of approximately 8 workshops (estimated 35-50 additional participants) that we cannot independently confirm.

---

## Assessment of HQ Tracking System

The GlobalBridge HQ tracking system captures formal code-compliant activities only and is not designed to capture community-based informal delivery. The 45% dashboard figure does not reflect overall program progress; it reflects the subset of activities that met the HQ system's coding requirements. We do not treat the dashboard figure as a complete picture of program delivery.

The tracking system's limitations are a systemic design issue affecting at least two field offices (Nairobi and Dhaka, per field staff reports). We recommend a system redesign for Year 3 that accommodates community-based delivery models.

---

## Recommendation

1. For the formal Pemberton submission, use the conservative verified figure of 58% (or inclusive 63%)
2. Pursue retroactive documentation for the 8 unverified workshops (community leader sign-offs, facilitator statements)
3. Address the HQ tracking system design limitation in the Year 3 documentation improvement plan
4. Obtain government co-signatures for the three infrastructure projects as soon as possible
