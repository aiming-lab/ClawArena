# Pemberton Foundation Grant Agreement -- GlobalBridge Foundation
## Year 2 Compliance Provisions Excerpt

**Excerpted by:** Rachel Wu, Finance Director, GlobalBridge Foundation HQ
**Grant Reference:** PEM-2024-GB-001
**Grant Period:** January 2024 -- December 2026 (3 years)
**Total Grant Value:** $2,800,000

### Section 4.2: Deliverable Reporting

All deliverables must be documented in the grantee's approved monitoring and evaluation system and submitted with supporting evidence as specified in Annex C. Deliverable reporting shall be submitted quarterly using the Pemberton standard reporting template. The grantee's M&E system must produce verifiable records of all claimed activities. The Pemberton Grants Management Committee reserves the right to commission independent verification of any reported deliverable.

### Section 4.3: Independent Evaluation

Pemberton may request or the grantee may commission an independent evaluation at any point during the grant period. Independent evaluation findings carry equal evidentiary weight to grantee-reported data for compliance assessment purposes. Where independent evaluation findings differ from grantee-reported figures, the Grants Management Committee will determine which figure to use for formal compliance determination.

### Section 6.1: Budget Flexibility

Grantee may reallocate up to 15% of any approved budget line without prior written approval from Pemberton Grants Management, provided the reallocation supports grant objectives and is documented in the next quarterly financial report. Reallocations exceeding 15% of any single budget line require prior written approval from Pemberton Grants Management before the expenditure is incurred. Failure to obtain prior written approval for reallocations above 15% constitutes a compliance breach requiring a formal waiver application.

### Section 6.2: Budget Monitoring

The grantee shall submit quarterly financial reports comparing approved budget to actual expenditure by budget line. Variances exceeding 10% on any budget line must be accompanied by written justification.

### Section 6.3: Budget Variance Reporting

Any budget line variance exceeding 10% at the time of mid-term review shall be reported with written justification including: (a) operational rationale for the variance, (b) impact on grant objectives, and (c) proposed corrective action or waiver request. Budget line variances exceeding 15% without prior written approval require a formal waiver application.

### Section 9.1: Mid-Term Review

Pemberton reserves the right to conduct an unscheduled mid-term review at any point during the grant period. Upon notification of a mid-term review, the grantee must provide all requested documentation within 14 calendar days. The mid-term review may include: quantitative deliverable dashboards, financial tracking reports, field narrative reports, independent evaluation materials, and any other documentation the Grants Management Committee deems relevant.

### Section 9.2: Review Standards

The Grants Management Committee will assess deliverable completion using the best available evidence, including both grantee-reported data and independent evaluation findings where available. The Committee may apply a strict or flexible interpretation of deliverable documentation requirements at its discretion, consistent with Pemberton governance standards.

---

### Section 11.1: Performance Assessment

At the mid-term review, deliverable completion is assessed against mid-point proxy targets as defined in the approved work plan. Mid-point proxy targets represent 50% of Year 2 annual targets for each deliverable category.

### Section 11.2: Remediation Plan

Where the mid-term review identifies material shortfalls (defined as overall deliverable completion below 60% at mid-point), Pemberton may require a formal remediation plan within 30 days. The remediation plan must include: (a) root cause analysis of the shortfall, (b) specific corrective actions with responsible parties and deadlines, (c) revised targets for the remainder of the grant period, and (d) documentation improvement commitments where applicable.

### Section 12.1: Grant Recall

Pemberton reserves the right to recall uncommitted grant funds where the grantee fails to submit a remediation plan within the specified timeframe or where the remediation plan is assessed as inadequate by the Grants Management Committee. Grant recall provisions are exercised only after the grantee has been given reasonable opportunity to address identified shortfalls.
