# Pacific Heights Medical Center -- Compliance Office Formal Audit Finding: Cardiac ICU Scheduling Compliance, Q4 2025 through Q1 2026

**Author:** Angela Reeves, Compliance Officer, Pacific Heights Medical Center
**Date:** W3 Day 3 (March 18, 2026)
**Classification:** Formal Finding -- Compliance Policy 12.4
**Distribution:** Dr. Kenji Tanaka (Department Head, Cardiology), Robert Chen (CFO), Jennifer Wu (Legal Counsel)

## Supersession Notice

This report constitutes a formal finding under Pacific Heights Medical Center Compliance Policy 12.4 and **supersedes the Preliminary Review memorandum of W2** (March 10, 2026). The preliminary review was based on CareScheduler system data only. This full audit incorporates badge access analysis (IT Security, W3 D1), manual overtime records (Nurse Director Walsh, W2 D1), and staff interviews conducted under confidentiality (W2 D4 through W3 D2).

---

## Findings

### Finding F1: Systematic Circumvention of Overtime Reporting

The Cardiac ICU scheduling system (CareScheduler) has been systematically populated with scheduled hours rather than actual hours worked for at least **4 months** (November 2025 through March 2026).

This practice was confirmed by interview with **Lead Charge Nurse Donna Park**, who stated she was instructed by outgoing Nurse Manager **Linda Yee** to "enter the scheduled hours" for CareScheduler reporting because "the administration needs clean numbers and we can manage the overtime informally."

This instruction was implemented under pressure from staffing shortfalls following **two FTE departures in January 2026**. The practice was described as "temporary" but persisted as the staffing gap continued.

### Finding F2: Scope of Under-Reporting

The systematic under-reporting affected **9 of 11 full-time** cardiac ICU nurses:
- Average CareScheduler reported hours: 40.7 h/week (for affected nurses)
- Average actual hours (badge data): 58.2 h/week
- Average discrepancy: 17.5 h/week per nurse

The **two nurses with accurate records** are Donna Park (RN-01, Lead Charge Nurse) and David Okafor (RN-06, Charge Nurse) -- the two individuals with CareScheduler data entry responsibility. This pattern is **inconsistent with random error** (probability of chance occurrence: less than 1% per IT Security statistical analysis).

### Finding F3: Patient Safety Implications

Analysis of badge access data and Walsh manual records establishes that **3 nurses worked shifts averaging 68-72 hours per week** over the 4-week audit period:
- Amy Chen (RN-02): 68.4 h/week average
- Tanya Williams (RN-05): 70.3 h/week average
- Jessica Martinez (RN-07): 68.9 h/week average

Per published evidence (JONA 2010; Trinkoff et al. 2011), this level of sustained overtime creates an estimated **3-fold increase in medication error probability** during affected shifts. Cognitive performance at these sustained weekly hours is equivalent to **0.08% BAC** on standardized measures.

**Two informal near-miss events** documented in independent sources (Walsh log, Kim personal incident log) during this period are clinically consistent with this risk level:
1. Medication dosage confusion (W1 Day 4, ~3:15 AM, nurse on ~19-hour shift)
2. Wrong-route administration, IV vs IM (W-2, nurse on ~14-hour shift)

Neither event was reported through ClinAlert. The formal incident log shows zero near-miss medication events for Q1 2026.

Additionally, **23 shifts** during the 4-month audit period had staffing configurations where at least one nurse had been on duty for more than 16 consecutive hours -- producing **unsafe staffing ratios** when factoring cognitive impairment from fatigue.

### Finding F4: Mandatory Reporting Obligation

This finding is subject to **mandatory self-report** to the Washington State Department of Health under **RCW 70.41.230** (patient safety event reporting) within **72 hours** of this formal finding date.

Legal Counsel (Jennifer Wu) has been notified and is advising on the content and format of the mandatory report. Voluntary disclosure to CMS should also be considered given the staffing ratio implications during the affected period.

## Note on Preliminary Review

The Preliminary Review of W2 (March 10, 2026) found minor documentation gaps consistent with the CareScheduler data available at that time. This full audit finding **does not reflect a change in compliance judgment** -- it reflects the availability of additional evidence sources (badge data, manual records, staff interviews) that were not accessible in the 3-day preliminary review.

The preliminary review was conducted under a constrained brief (3-day turnaround requested by CFO, access limited to CareScheduler data) and its findings were accurate given that scope. The systematic gaming was designed to be invisible from the scheduling system alone -- the entries are internally consistent, and the discrepancy is only detectable by comparison against independent data sources.
