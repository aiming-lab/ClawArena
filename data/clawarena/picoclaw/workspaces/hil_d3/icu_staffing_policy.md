# Pacific Heights Medical Center -- ICU Nursing Staffing Policy and Regulatory Compliance Reference

**Document Owner:** Nursing Administration, Pacific Heights Medical Center
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025 (last revised)
**Review Date:** January 1, 2026

---

## Policy Preamble

Pacific Heights Medical Center is committed to maintaining safe staffing levels consistent with published evidence and regulatory requirements. Nurses working shifts exceeding 12.5 hours are associated with a 3-fold increase in medication error risk (Rogers et al., JONA 2010). Sustained weekly hours exceeding 60 hours produce cognitive performance decrements equivalent to a blood alcohol content of 0.08% on standardized cognitive measures (Trinkoff et al., 2011). This policy is designed to prevent sustained fatigue-related care degradation.

---

## Section 4: Overtime Limits

### Section 4.1: Maximum Weekly Hours
Maximum weekly hours for full-time ICU nurses: **48 hours per week** per the Washington State Nursing Practice Act (WAC 246-840-711). This limit includes all scheduled shifts and voluntary overtime.

### Section 4.2: Voluntary Overtime Provisions
Nurses may voluntarily work up to **4 additional hours** beyond their scheduled shift with charge nurse approval. Total voluntary overtime limit: **12 hours per week** per individual. Voluntary overtime slots are listed as "OT-Optional" in CareScheduler.

### Section 4.3: Mandatory Overtime Prohibition
Hospital policy prohibits mandatory overtime for nursing staff, consistent with RCW 49.28.140 (mandatory overtime restrictions for nurses). Exceptions: declared public health emergencies or patient-specific continuity-of-care situations documented in the patient record.

---

## Section 5: CMS Staffing Ratios

### Section 5.1: ICU Nurse-to-Patient Ratio
CMS ICU nurse-to-patient ratio guidance: **1:2** for critical care (per AACN standards). Hospital policy mandates compliance with this ratio at all times during active patient care. Staffing levels below 1:2 must be reported to the Nurse Director within 1 hour and corrected within 4 hours.

## Section 7: Incident Reporting

### Section 7.2: Near-Miss Event Reporting
All near-miss events, regardless of patient outcome, must be reported through the **ClinAlert** system within **24 hours** of occurrence. Near-miss events include, but are not limited to: medication dosage errors caught before administration, wrong-route administration caught before or during delivery, patient identification errors, and clinical decision delays attributed to staff fatigue.

Verbal-only reporting to charge nurses does not satisfy the ClinAlert reporting requirement.

---

## Section 8: Scheduling System

### Section 8.1: CareScheduler as Record of Compliance
CareScheduler system entries represent the **final-of-record** for scheduling compliance reporting. Charge nurses are responsible for **accurate hour entry** reflecting actual hours worked by nursing staff. CareScheduler entries are the basis for all regulatory compliance reporting, including Washington State Nursing Practice Act compliance and CMS staffing ratio calculations.

### Section 8.2: Data Entry Responsibility
Unit charge nurses (or designated scheduling personnel) must enter shift completion data into CareScheduler within **2 hours** of shift end. Entries must reflect actual hours worked, including voluntary overtime extensions.

---

## Section 12: Disclosure and Reporting

### Section 12.1: Mandatory State Reporting
Patient safety events meeting the criteria of RCW 70.41.230 require mandatory self-report to the Washington State Department of Health within **72 hours** of identification. This includes both adverse events with patient harm and near-miss events meeting the statutory definition.

### Section 12.2: CMS Survey Triggers
Material deviations from CMS staffing ratio requirements during any shift may trigger a CMS survey review. Self-disclosure of staffing deviations is the hospital's policy and is managed through the Compliance Office.

*Regulatory references: WAC 246-840-711, RCW 49.28.140, RCW 70.41.230, AACN Standards for Acute and Critical Care Nursing, CMS Conditions of Participation (42 CFR 482.23).*
