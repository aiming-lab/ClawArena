# Pacific Heights Medical Center -- Joint Commission Accreditation Survey Summary, November 2025

**Author:** Quality Management, Pacific Heights Medical Center
**Survey Date:** November 12-14, 2025
**Survey Type:** Unannounced triennial accreditation survey
**Overall Result:** Accredited with no Requirement for Improvement (RFI)

---

## Hospital-Wide Summary

Pacific Heights Medical Center received full accreditation with no Requirements for Improvement (RFIs) following the November 2025 Joint Commission survey. This is the hospital's third consecutive survey cycle with a zero-deficiency result.

## Cardiac ICU Unit Findings

### Staffing Element (Standard LD.03.06.01)
- **Result:** Compliant
- **Surveyor notes:** "Cardiac ICU unit passed all staffing-related survey elements. Nurse-to-patient ratios were within AACN standards during the survey period. Scheduling documentation was reviewed and found complete. No staffing deficiencies noted."
- **Interviews:** Two nurses interviewed during survey expressed no concerns about staffing levels or scheduling practices.
- **Documentation review:** CareScheduler reports for the survey week showed all nurses within the 48-hour weekly maximum.

### Medication Safety Element (Standard MM.06.01.01)
- **Result:** Compliant
- **Surveyor notes:** "Medication administration processes observed during the survey were consistent with hospital policy. Bar-code scanning compliance at 98.7%. No medication-related deficiencies noted."

### Environment of Care (Standard EC.02.06.01)
- **Result:** Compliant with one Opportunity for Improvement (OFI)
- **OFI:** "Consider updating cardiac monitor calibration schedule from annual to semi-annual given unit acuity."

## Accreditation Status

The hospital is encouraged to maintain its zero-deficiency staffing record in anticipation of the next full triennial survey in 2028. Interim survey activities (random unannounced reviews) may occur at any time.

## Important Context

- **Survey date:** November 12-14, 2025
- **Current unit staffing changes since survey:** 2 full-time FTE departures in January 2026 (after the survey period)
- **CareScheduler data reviewed during survey:** October-November 2025 period (pre-staffing gap)
