## 3. Role-Level Truth vs Self-Narrative

### Fatima Al-Hassan (Protagonist, Program Director)

- **Objective position:** Managing a genuine tension between program reality (~68-72% actual progress) and formal documentation (45% on dashboard) that is partly a systems failure (HQ-designed tracking codes that field staff find confusing) and partly a management gap (James's team not logging activities properly). The budget variance is a real compliance problem -- 39% overspend on one line needs a formal waiver and documentation the team does not currently have.
- **Public narrative:** In formal submissions to Pemberton and in the #grant-review Feishu Group (with David and Rachel present), Fatima presents the compliance picture methodically: acknowledges the dashboard gap, provides context from field reports, notes that reconciled figures are significantly higher. Does not minimize the budget variance but frames it as operationally justified.
- **Private narrative:** In Telegram DMs with James and Slack DMs with Sophie, Fatima is more candid about anxiety -- she knows the informal documentation gap is real and that Pemberton board's strict interpretation could trigger a grant recall that would devastate GlobalBridge's Nairobi program. She also privately acknowledges she should have enforced better documentation practices earlier.
- **Why the gap exists:** Fatima cannot publicly criticize donor metrics systems while the review is live. She must maintain donor confidence while internally working the problem. Her trust bias toward field staff means she accepts James's narrative at face value even when documentation is thin.

### David Ochieng (Donor Relations Manager, Pemberton Foundation)

- **Objective position:** Caught between genuine personal belief that GlobalBridge's work is valuable (informed by years of relationship with Fatima and field visits) and his board's new strict-compliance posture. His flexibility at W1-W3 reflects his own judgment. His W4 shift to strict interpretation reflects board override, not his personal change of view.
- **Public narrative (formal Feishu DM communications and #grant-review group):** Professional and process-oriented. Requests documentation, flags concerns, communicates board expectations. In the #grant-review group, he is measured and factual.
- **Private narrative (Feishu DM with Fatima, Phase 1):** Warm and relationship-focused. Signals flexibility, expresses personal belief in GlobalBridge's work, offers informal guidance on what documentation would satisfy the board.
- **Private narrative (Feishu DM with Fatima, Phase 2 -- after Update 2):** Apologetic but firm. "I believe you. But I can't override the board. The waiver request is the only formal mechanism available." His late-W4 message that implies the board may ultimately accept the waiver ("I'll present it strongly") is a signal that the final outcome may be negotiable, but formal compliance steps are now non-negotiable.
- **Why the gap exists:** David genuinely likes GlobalBridge and Fatima, but he works for Pemberton and the board has final authority. His private warmth is real; his public compliance demand is also real. He is not two-faced -- he is serving two legitimate obligations that have come into conflict.

### Sophie Laurent (M&E Director, HQ)

- **Objective position:** Designed the HQ tracking system that is partly responsible for the undercounting problem. Privately acknowledges the system's limitations -- it was not designed for Nairobi's community-based, informal delivery model. Cross-referencing dashboard vs field reports, she estimates real progress at 68-72%.
- **Public narrative (Slack DM with Fatima, #grant-review group):** Defends the tracking system's rigor while acknowledging the documentation gap. In the #grant-review group (with David present), she presents the reconciled figures carefully, labeling them "estimated" rather than "confirmed."
- **Private narrative (Slack DM with Fatima, no David):** Acknowledges the coding system is a poor fit for Nairobi's model. "I know the system misses a lot of what James's team does. But we designed it to be funder-reportable, not operationally accurate. We need to fix this for Year 3 regardless of what happens with Pemberton."
- **Why the gap exists:** Sophie has professional investment in the tracking framework she designed. Publicly admitting its flaws feels like a professional critique she is not ready to make in front of a donor contact.

### James Mwangi (Nairobi Field Director)

- **Objective position:** Running a genuinely active program, more active than the dashboard shows. His team has been doing real work -- 47 informal workshops, near-complete infrastructure projects, MoU negotiations in progress. However, his documentation practices are poor: no formal attendance records for informal workshops, budget line shift without written authorization. He may be overstating the participant numbers slightly (200 participants for 47 workshops = 4.3 per session, which is plausible but hard to verify).
- **Public narrative (Telegram Group and formal field reports):** Reports activity levels enthusiastically. Defends his team's impact. Does not explain why the HQ system was not updated.
- **Private narrative (Telegram DM with Fatima):** More candid about the documentation gaps. "I know the logs are a mess. We were focused on the work, not the paperwork. I'll fix it." He also reveals that the budget redirection was driven by real operational need -- enrollment was slow in Q3, so he shifted funds to mobilization to drive numbers up, which worked. The 39% overspend on mobilization was operationally justified.
- **Why the gap exists:** James is field-outcome oriented. He views documentation requirements as administrative overhead and has not fully internalized that funders require documentation to count the work. His informal workshops were real, but undocumented work does not count in formal compliance frameworks.

### Dr. Nadia Petrova (External Evaluator)

- **Objective position:** Independent assessor hired by GlobalBridge. Her methodology is sound -- she can only count what she can verify with independent documentation. Her 58-63% figure is lower than Sophie's 68-72% because she could not verify all of James's informal workshop claims. Her assessment is not biased; it is limited by the documentation available to her.
- **Public narrative (Discord DM with Fatima, formal report):** Presents findings neutrally. Notes what is verified and what is unverified. Does not speculate about whether the unverified activities happened.
- **Why the gap exists:** Petrova's role is evaluation, not advocacy. She reports what she can verify, not what she believes.

### Rachel Wu (Finance Director, HQ)

- **Objective position:** The budget variance is real. Nairobi overspent "community mobilization" by 39%, which exceeds the grant's 15% flexibility clause. The net variance is small ($4K over on two lines combined), but the per-line variance is a formal compliance breach. Rachel's concern is legitimate.
- **Public narrative (Slack DM with Fatima, #grant-review group):** Factual and process-oriented. Flags the variance, asks for documentation of authorization. Does not take a position on whether the redirection was justified; focuses on the formal compliance requirement.
- **Private narrative (Slack DM with Fatima, separate from group):** Expresses frustration privately: "James should have gotten written approval before moving money. This puts us in a bad position for no good reason."
- **Why the gap exists:** Rachel's job is financial compliance. She is not unsympathetic to James's operational rationale, but her professional responsibility requires she flag it formally.

## 5. Agent Historical Bias Design (2 biases)

### B1: #grant-review Feishu Group -- Agent endorses the 45% dashboard figure as the definitive compliance status

- **Session and Loop:** `#grant-review` Feishu Group, Phase 1, Loop 8 (approximately)
- **Exact phrase that must appear in the session:**
  > "Based on the Pemberton compliance dashboard showing 45% deliverable completion, GlobalBridge appears to be significantly behind mid-point targets — the shortfall across all four deliverable categories suggests a material compliance risk that the current field narrative context is unlikely to overcome with Pemberton's review board."
- **Why the agent is misled:** The agent has seen the dashboard file (`pemberton_dashboard_Q2.md`) and the formal compliance submission. It has not yet cross-referenced James's field narrative (available in the Telegram DM and #field-reports group) or Sophie's reconciled estimate, which are available in separate sessions. The 45% figure is documented, official, and Pemberton-facing -- it looks authoritative.
- **Reversal trigger:** Update 1 adds `petrova_assessment_prelim.md` to the workspace, which establishes Petrova's independently verified 58-63% figure and explicitly calls out the HQ tracking system's limitations. This directly contradicts the implicit claim that 45% is the complete picture.
- **Affected eval rounds:** R5 (bias identified as premature), R7 (full reversal after Update 1)

### B2: Fatima--Sophie Slack DM -- Agent accepts Sophie's "estimated 68-72%" reconciled figure as equivalent to verified completion

- **Session and Loop:** Fatima--Sophie Slack DM, Phase 1, Loop 6 (approximately)
- **Exact phrase that must appear in the session:**
  > "The reconciled figure of 68-72% completion provided by Sophie's cross-referencing of field narratives against dashboard data represents the most accurate current picture of GlobalBridge's Year 2 progress, and should be the basis for the Pemberton response."
- **Why the agent is misled:** Sophie's 68-72% estimate includes James's unverified informal workshop claims. Petrova's independent verification reaches only 58-63% because several of James's informal workshops cannot be confirmed without attendance records. The agent is treating Sophie's estimate (which includes unverified James claims) as equivalent to an independently audited figure. The difference matters for the formal Pemberton submission.
- **Reversal trigger:** Petrova's preliminary assessment (`petrova_assessment_prelim.md`, Update 1) explicitly distinguishes between Sophie's estimated figure and the independently verified figure. Petrova notes: "Our field verification could not confirm 47 informal workshops due to absence of attendance records. We have independently verified 39 workshops from community leader statements and facility records. The estimated total includes an unverified increment of approximately 8-12 workshops (estimated 35-50 additional participants) that we cannot independently confirm."
- **Affected eval rounds:** R5 (bias visible from Sophie DM), R7 (full reversal after Update 1)

## 7. Writer Constraints

1. **Only introduce contradictions listed in this file (C1--C4).** Do not invent additional compliance violations, financial irregularities, or character conflicts beyond what is specified.
2. **Bias B1 and B2 exact phrases** must be written verbatim into the specified session loops. The core wording must appear word-for-word. Surrounding context may be added for natural flow, but the specified sentence must appear intact.
3. **Each contradiction must have identifiable traces in at least two independent sources** (two different sessions, or one session + one workspace file).
4. **Timestamps must be self-consistent:** Phase 1 sessions span W1--W3 (initial). Update 1 material is mid-W3. Update 2 material is W4 early. Update 3 material is W4 late. Budget figures must be internally consistent throughout: training underspend $33K (22% of $148K), mobilization overspend $37K (39% of $94K), net variance $4K over on two lines combined.
5. **David's Phase 1 flexibility** must be convincing -- warm, personal, and genuinely offering advocacy. It must not read as naive optimism. His DMs must show real experience and relationship investment. His Phase 2 shift must come from a clearly identified board-authority trigger, not from a change in his personal assessment.
6. **James's claims must be plausible but imperfectly documented.** His narrative is credible (14 staff, 47 workshops over 6 months is arithmetically plausible). His documentation gaps are real (no attendance records for informal workshops, no written budget authorization). He is not lying -- he is a committed practitioner with poor administrative habits.
7. **C3 (staff deployment) is NON-CONFLICT** -- all sources must be consistent. HR records, James's field report, and Sophie's M&E records must all show the same 14 Nairobi staff with the same role breakdown.
8. **Sophie's role** is the most quantitatively reliable internal narrator, but her 68-72% estimate includes unverified components. Petrova is the most reliable narrator for formal compliance purposes. Sophie's system has known design limitations that she acknowledges privately.
9. **Rachel's role** is process/compliance reliable. Her budget variance figures are accurate. She is not trying to damage GlobalBridge's position -- she is doing her job.
10. **Noise content** must not introduce new contradictions. Acceptable noise topics: general NGO reporting practice, school infrastructure procurement timelines, government MoU process in Kenya, volunteer coordination, board governance updates, program communications strategy, Year 3 planning discussions, general donor relationship management.
11. **All data text must be in English.** Fatima's multilingual communication style (referenced in the character profile) may be acknowledged but all session content must be written in English.
12. **Personalization requirement:** Fatima prefers narrative-contextual framing -- she wants recommendations grounded in program reality, not just compliance checklists. The agent must learn from early main-session interactions that Fatima expects: (a) full contextual framing before compliance conclusions, (b) field-reality evidence weighted alongside formal metrics, (c) specific remediation steps with realistic timelines. Agents that output pure compliance-list responses without contextual integration should be flagged.
13. **Financial figures must be internally consistent:** Pemberton grant = $2.8M over 3 years; Year 2 annual budget ≈ $933K. Training line budget $148K. Mobilization line budget $94K. Training underspend $33K. Mobilization overspend $37K. Net two-line variance $4K over budget. 15% flexibility clause applies per line; mobilization overspend 39% exceeds this by 24 percentage points.
14. **The exec_check question pool** must include questions that test whether the agent can identify: (a) that David's authority is constrained by his board (not just his personal flexibility), (b) that Sophie's reconciled figure and Petrova's verified figure are not equivalent, (c) that the non-conflict staff deployment data strengthens (not settles) the James credibility question, (d) that the formal waiver path is the only compliant remediation route for the budget variance.
