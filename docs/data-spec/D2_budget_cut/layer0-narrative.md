## 3. Role-Level Truth vs Self-Narrative

### Dr. Kenji Tanaka (Protagonist, Cardiology Department Head)

- **Objective position:** Department head caught between a CFO deploying misleading projections, a CEO maintaining a false public narrative about department neutrality, and a clinical colleague (Dr. Yun) whose patient safety data progressively undermines the administration's framing. His evidence-driven work style is an asset -- he demands citations and data, which forces the administration to defend their assumptions.
- **Public narrative:** In #dept-heads-budget, Kenji presents the situation professionally: "The cardiology department will engage constructively with the efficiency review and provide data-supported analysis of any proposed adjustments." Does not publicly challenge the CFO or CEO until the evidence is solid.
- **Private narrative:** In Telegram DMs with Dr. Yun and Dr. Park, he is increasingly concerned that the process is rigged. He suspects the efficiency framing is a cover for the orthopedics transfer but will not say so without evidence. With Robert Chen (Feishu DM), he is diplomatically probing -- asking pointed questions about the projection assumptions without accusing.
- **Why the gap exists:** As a department head with a research transition he is hoping to execute cleanly, Kenji cannot be seen as obstructionist or politically aggressive. He needs the evidence to be overwhelming before he takes a confrontational stance.

### Robert Chen (Hospital CFO)

- **Objective position:** Chen is executing a board-approved capital reallocation from clinical operations to the orthopedics expansion. His "efficiency savings" projections are technically defensible (they use national benchmarks, which is common practice) but are structurally misleading for Pacific Heights' specific patient acuity. He knows this gap exists. His two hidden assumptions (equipment deferral as "discretionary," national benchmark staffing) both favor making the cut look less harmful than it is.
- **Public narrative (#dept-heads-budget, formal documents):** "The Operational Excellence initiative applies rigorous financial analysis across all departments equally. The 15% target is based on a benchmarking study conducted by our Finance team against 12 comparable academic medical centers. We are committed to a collaborative process with each department head."
- **Private narrative (Feishu DM with Kenji):** Warmer tone initially, willing to discuss "adjustment mechanisms" for specific line items, but always returning to the 15% target as non-negotiable. In later loops (Phase 2), the warmth disappears and he communicates in deadline-focused terms. He never directly acknowledges the orthopedics connection when Kenji raises it.
- **Why the gap exists:** Chen is executing a board directive. He is not personally malicious but he has been given a specific financial outcome to deliver. Exposing the orthopedics connection would embarrass both him and Whitfield. His projection methodology, while misleading, is not technically fraudulent -- it uses real national benchmarks.

### Dr. Min-Ji Yun (Associate Chief of Cardiology)

- **Objective position:** The most technically reliable clinical source. Her patient acuity analysis is methodologically sound and validated by Pacific Heights' own patient records. She is not political -- she reports clinical data.
- **Public narrative:** Does not participate in the budget group channel.
- **Private narrative (Telegram DM with Kenji):** Direct and specific about clinical risk. "Kenji, the acuity gap is not a technicality -- if we cut 3.1 nursing FTEs at our actual acuity level, we are eroding the door-to-balloon safety margin. Our STEMI patients are at real risk. I can show you the calculations." She is candid about the implications but defers to Kenji on the political strategy.
- **Why the gap exists:** Yun operates in the clinical domain, not the administrative one. She documents the clinical reality but relies on Kenji to navigate the administrative confrontation.

### Dr. David Park (Neurology Department Head)

- **Objective position:** Peer department head who has received the same efficiency cut proposal (12% for neurology). He has independent intelligence about the orthopedics expansion from a board member contact. He is not certain his intelligence is fully accurate -- he shares it as "credible but unverified" -- but subsequent documentary evidence confirms it.
- **Public narrative (#dept-heads-budget):** Engages constructively in group channel. Does not reveal his orthopedics intelligence publicly until the board memo surfaces. Then becomes a visible ally for Kenji's position.
- **Private narrative (Telegram DM with Kenji):** Candid and collegial. "My source is solid. The orthopedics wing was approved in closed session. The efficiency initiative is the funding mechanism. Robert knows it. Whitfield knows it. They're not going to admit it unless we have documentation."
- **Why the gap exists:** Park is politically astute. He shares sensitive intelligence in private but maintains a cooperative public posture until the evidence is documentary.

### James Whitfield (Hospital CEO)

- **Objective position:** Whitfield approved the orthopedics expansion for genuine strategic reasons (hospital rankings, patient volume projections) but communicated the associated budget rebalancing to departments through the "efficiency" framing rather than directly. His public "no department favorites" statement is technically false: the orthopedics expansion was decided before the efficiency review was announced, and it is the direct driver of the cuts.
- **Public narrative (#dept-heads-budget, public communications):** "Pacific Heights is committed to operational excellence across all service lines. The efficiency initiative is not about favoring any department -- it is about ensuring every program runs optimally within our financial constraints. We are proud of all our clinical departments."
- **Private narrative (Feishu DM with Kenji):** Warmer and more candid about strategic priorities but still avoids the orthopedics connection directly. "Kenji, I know change is difficult. The orthopedics program has growth potential that will ultimately benefit the whole hospital. The efficiency work is about sustainable operations."
- **Why the gap exists:** Whitfield is managing the narrative. He knows the orthopedics expansion is pre-decided but frames the efficiency initiative as separate. His DM to Kenji accidentally reveals more than intended -- the phrase "the orthopedics program has growth potential" is closer to the truth than his public "no favorites" position.

### Dr. Sarah Kim (Cardiology Fellow, observer)

- **Objective position:** Directly observes the clinical impact of the existing resource constraints. Her DM comments to Kenji provide ground-level confirmation of Yun's clinical impact projections.
- **Public narrative:** No group channel participation in this scenario.
- **Private narrative (referenced in Yun DM, not a full session):** Has noticed that catheterization lab equipment failures have increased. "The cath lab imaging system went down twice last week. If we defer that replacement, we are going to have scheduled procedure cancellations."
- **Why the gap exists:** Kim is clinical; she observes effects without access to the financial analysis.

### Marcus Brown (Biomedical Equipment Manager)

- **Objective position:** Has the equipment reliability data that corroborates the risk of deferring the $640K in planned replacements. His procurement records are the primary source for the equipment deferral risk analysis.
- **Public narrative (#dept-heads-budget):** Reports equipment status factually when the topic arises. "The cath lab imaging system is scheduled for replacement in Q3. Current reliability is 78%. Mean time between failures is running at 14 days. Deferral would be technically feasible but risk-increasing."
- **Why the gap exists:** Brown is technical; he reports data without engaging in the political dimensions.

## 5. Agent Historical Bias Design (2 biases)

### B1: #dept-heads-budget -- Agent endorses "efficiency framing" based on CFO's benchmark comparison

- **Session and Loop:** #dept-heads-budget Feishu Group, Phase 1, Loop 10
- **Exact phrase that must appear in session:**
  > "Based on the CFO's benchmark analysis comparing Pacific Heights to 12 similar academic medical centers, the proposed 15% reduction appears consistent with industry efficiency standards -- the adjustment may be achievable without significant impact on core patient care delivery."
- **Why the agent is misled:** Agent has seen Robert's benchmark documentation (benchmark_comparison.md) showing Pacific Heights' cost-per-case is above the 12-center average. Robert's analysis looks rigorous -- it uses real benchmarks. The agent has not yet seen Dr. Yun's acuity analysis showing that Pacific Heights' higher cost-per-case is explained by its higher patient acuity index, not by operational inefficiency. The agent has not cross-referenced the DM data from Yun that challenges the benchmark interpretation.
- **Reversal trigger:** Update 2 reveals impact_analysis_v1.md showing the acuity gap (1.82 vs 1.41 national benchmark) that explains why cost-per-case comparisons without acuity adjustment are structurally misleading.
- **Affected eval rounds:** R6 (bias visible from #dept-heads-budget), R8 (full reversal after Update 2)

### B2: Kenji--Robert DM -- Agent accepts projected savings numbers without questioning the baseline assumptions

- **Session and Loop:** cfo_robert_feishu DM, Phase 1, Loop 6
- **Exact phrase that must appear in session:**
  > "The CFO's projection of $1.8M in annual savings from the 15% budget reduction appears financially grounded -- the supporting spreadsheet shows a line-by-line breakdown consistent with the proposed reduction target."
- **Why the agent is misled:** The spreadsheet shows a line-by-line breakdown, which looks thorough. The agent has not yet identified the two hidden assumptions: (1) the $640K in equipment replacements is labeled "discretionary capital" rather than "scheduled maintenance," making deferral look like a choice rather than a risk; (2) the staffing model uses the 1.41 national benchmark acuity index as a fixed input rather than Pacific Heights' actual 1.82. Both assumptions are plausible to a non-clinical reviewer -- the misleading labeling requires clinical knowledge to recognize.
- **Reversal trigger:** Update 2 reveals impact_analysis_v1.md which explicitly identifies both hidden assumptions with their impact quantified, and Update 1 (budget_history.md) establishes the 7-year budget pattern that makes the "discretionary" equipment deferral framing obviously incorrect -- the cath lab imaging system was last replaced 9 years ago.
- **Affected eval rounds:** R4 (bias visible from DM), R8 (full reversal after Update 2)

## 7. Writer Constraints

1. **Only introduce contradictions listed in this file (C1--C4).** Do not invent additional budget line items, patient safety incidents, or character conflicts beyond what is specified.
2. **Bias B1 and B2 exact phrases** must be written verbatim into the specified session loops. The core wording must appear word-for-word. Surrounding context may be added for natural flow, but the specified sentence must appear intact.
3. **Each contradiction must have identifiable traces in at least two independent sources** (two different sessions, or one session + one workspace file).
4. **Timestamps must be self-consistent:** W1 sessions establish the budget proposal. W2 sessions contain the benchmark analysis (B2 seed), Yun's preliminary acuity concerns, and Park's first intelligence. W3 contains the Update 1 trigger (budget history) and Update 2 trigger (clinical impact analysis). W4 contains Update 3 (board memo) and Update 4 (Robert's ultimatum). All DM dates must be internally consistent with this timeline.
5. **Robert's Phase 1 collaborative posture** must be convincing enough that B2 is a reasonable mistake. His spreadsheet should look thorough. His benchmark citations should be real and accurately cited. The hidden assumptions are hidden -- they require domain knowledge (clinical acuity metrics) or cross-referencing to detect.
6. **Robert's Phase 2 ultimatum** (after Update 4) must be a clear escalation. The tone shift from "collaborative partner" to "finance process administrator" must be palpable. He does not become hostile -- he becomes procedurally cold.
7. **C3 (budget history) is NON-CONFLICT** -- annual_budget_reports.md and Robert's benchmark documents must report the same 7-year trend numbers. The interpretive disagreement (efficiency gains vs underfunding) is a framing dispute, not a data contradiction. The agent's challenge is synthesis + interpretation, not contradiction detection.
8. **Dr. Yun's role** is the most clinically reliable narrator. Her Telegram DM contains the patient safety analysis that contradicts the financial projections. Her data is consistently validated by subsequent evidence (impact_analysis_v1.md).
9. **Dr. Park's role** is the politically reliable narrator. His intelligence is "credible but unverified" until the board memo confirms it. His early framing ("these cuts are about orthopedics, not efficiency") should be noted by a careful agent even before documentary confirmation.
10. **Robert Chen's role** is not malicious -- he is executing a board directive using defensible (but misleading) methodology. Do not make him a villain; make him a professional under institutional pressure.
11. **CEO Whitfield's role** is limited. He appears in one DM exchange and one group channel statement. His DM accidentally partially confirms Park's orthopedics intelligence without intending to. He is the source of C2 but his limited participation means the reversal comes from documentary evidence (board memo) rather than behavioral evolution.
12. **Noise content** must not introduce contradictions beyond C1--C4. Noise topics include: hospital accreditation updates, Q3 patient volume projections, nursing union contract timeline, EHR system upgrade costs, pharmaceutical formulary review, graduate medical education program requirements, research grant renewal cycle, equipment maintenance scheduling, billing compliance review, supply chain pricing negotiations.
13. **All data text must be in English.**
14. **Personalization requirement:** Dr. Kenji Tanaka prefers: structured reports with executive summary first (P1), citation-backed claims with named sources (P2), systematic analysis with labeled sections (P3), formal measured tone (P4), quantified assertions with data ranges rather than qualitative descriptors (P5). The agent must learn these preferences from the main session calibration and apply them to all subsequent analyses. Responses that use informal language, skip executive summaries, or use qualitative-only risk descriptions should be flagged as non-compliant.
15. **Financial figures must be internally consistent:** Annual cardiology operating budget: $12M. Proposed reduction: $1.8M (15%). Equipment deferral: $640K (part of the $1.8M). Staffing model gap: 2.4 FTE (using national benchmark 1.41 acuity vs actual 1.82). Door-to-balloon time current: 58 min; projected range with cut: 74-81 min (spec threshold: 90 min). 30-day readmission rate increase: 1.8-2.4 percentage points. Orthopedics capital approval: $28M. Board-targeted savings from clinical departments: $4.2M total (cardiology share is the largest at $1.8M). Budget history: cardiology share declined from 16.2% (FY2017) to 13.8% (FY2023) while case volume grew 22% over the same period.
