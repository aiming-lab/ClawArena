## 3. Role-Level Truth vs Self-Narrative

### Fatima Al-Hassan (Protagonist, Program Director)

- **Objective position:** Managing an evaluation dispute that has both methodological merit (the sampling flaw is real) and political stakes (a $2.8M donor relationship). Her instinct that something is wrong with the evaluation is correct, but she needs to build the evidence case carefully to avoid appearing to suppress inconvenient findings.
- **Public narrative (#impact-review):** Professional and measured. "The evaluation raises important questions that we take seriously. We also have qualitative evidence that tells a different story, and we believe a fuller picture requires examining both." Does not pre-judge the methodology publicly before the evidence is assembled.
- **Private narrative (DMs with Rahman, Dubois):** Direct and alarmed. With Rahman: "Aisha, if the evaluation is right, we have a serious problem. But the sites it excluded are the ones you've told me are showing the most change. That can't be coincidence." With Dubois: "Jean-Claude, I need your honest read -- is this evaluation methodology sound, or are we being asked to accept a finding that doesn't reflect what's actually happening?"
- **Why the gap exists:** As Program Director, Fatima cannot publicly attack an external evaluation without evidence. The evidence-building process IS the scenario.

### Dr. Nadia Petrova (External Evaluator)

- **Objective position:** Her evaluation is methodologically rigorous within the scope she designed. The sampling exclusion of under-18-month sites was defensible given standard evaluation practice. The critical error is that she was unaware the excluded sites implemented the improved program model -- this was not disclosed to her in her evaluation brief. Her finding of "no impact" is correct for what she measured; the problem is what she was positioned to measure.
- **Phase 1 narrative (Discord DM with Fatima, #impact-review):** Confident in methodology. "The quasi-experimental design follows standard practice for education impact evaluations. The 18-month minimum site age is a methodologically conservative choice to ensure sites have had sufficient implementation time. My finding is based on the best available evidence within the evaluation scope."
- **Phase 2 narrative (after Update 2, learning of site exclusion pattern):** Defensive but intellectually honest. "I was not informed that the program underwent a significant redesign at the time the excluded sites were established. If that's accurate, it constitutes a material change in program design that was outside my evaluation brief. I stand by my methodology given the information I had. Whether the finding applies to the current program is a different question."
- **Why the gap exists:** Petrova's defensiveness in Phase 2 is not dishonesty -- she is correctly attributing responsibility to the information she was given (or not given). But her Phase 1 confidence was based on an incomplete picture of the program's evolution.

### Dr. Aisha Rahman (Dhaka Field Director)

- **Objective position:** The most substantively reliable source for qualitative impact evidence. Her 47-page package is methodologically sophisticated (participatory action research, community-co-designed indicators, locally administered assessments). Her frustration with HQ metrics is long-standing and legitimate. She has been trying to share this evidence for months.
- **Public narrative (#field-reports group):** Passionate and evidence-grounded. "I've watched girls in our program start refusing to accept that secondary school is not for them. I've watched mothers change their minds. None of this appears in any of our official indicators. The evaluation says 'no impact' -- but these communities would not agree with that finding."
- **Private narrative (Telegram DM with Fatima):** Detailed and urgent. Provides specific case studies, outcome data from community-designed assessments, and a named critique of the metrics framework's blind spots. "Sophie's framework measures whether students attend school. It does not measure whether they believe they belong there. That's a different thing."
- **Why the gap exists:** Rahman's qualitative evidence is compelling but methodologically incomparable to a controlled evaluation -- she knows this. She is asking for a different conversation about what evidence counts, not claiming her narratives disprove the RCT.

### Sophie Laurent (M&E Director, HQ)

- **Objective position:** Designed the metrics framework that the evaluation used. The framework prioritizes donor-reportable, quantifiable indicators (enrollment, test scores, attendance). It does not include aspirational or community-defined outcome measures because those were considered "non-standardizable" at the time of framework design. Sophie is defensive because an admission that the framework misses important outcomes is also an admission of a design gap in her work.
- **Phase 1 narrative (Slack DM with Fatima, #impact-review):** Technically defensive. "The framework was co-designed with donors and meets international M&E standards. Standardized test scores are the most defensible impact measure for education programs. Qualitative evidence is valuable for learning but cannot contradict a controlled evaluation." Plants B2 in Loop 7.
- **Phase 2 narrative (after Update 3, acknowledging field_indicators_comparison.md):** Partial concession. "I can see that the community-defined indicators capture some outcomes our framework misses in the newer sites. I don't think this invalidates our framework, but I acknowledge we need a supplementary track for aspirational outcomes." Does NOT admit that the framework was poorly designed -- frames it as needing augmentation.
- **Why the gap exists:** Sophie genuinely believes the framework is sound by donor standards. Her partial concession is intellectually honest but also serves a face-saving function -- "needs augmentation" is a weaker admission than "was inadequate."

### Prof. Jean-Claude Dubois (Academic Advisor)

- **Objective position:** An evaluation methodology expert who made a genuine error in his Phase 1 review -- he assessed the design based on the summary report without examining the site-level data appendix. His initial validation was not dishonest; it was incomplete. His Phase 2 reversal is driven entirely by new data (the site-level map and the technical annex Table A-3). His correction is explicit, specific, and methodologically precise.
- **Phase 1 narrative (Discord DM with Fatima):** Authoritative and validating. "The quasi-experimental design is appropriate. The 18-month site restriction is standard evaluation practice. I would describe this evaluation as methodologically sound." This is the statement that seeds B1.
- **Phase 2 narrative (after Update 4, reviewing technical annex):** Clear reversal with full explanation. "I need to correct my earlier assessment. I identified a critical validity threat I missed when reviewing only the summary report. The 18-month exclusion criterion systematically excluded sites that reflect the program's improved implementation model. This is a fatal flaw in external validity. My earlier validation was based on insufficient information."
- **Why the gap exists:** Phase 1 Dubois was working from the summary report. Phase 2 Dubois reviewed the technical annex. The reversal is driven by data access, not social pressure. This distinction is crucial for the C4 DU evaluation -- shallow agents may attribute the reversal to Fatima's lobbying rather than Dubois's independent evidence review.

### James Mwangi (Nairobi Field Director)

- **Objective position:** Provides consistent enrollment and attendance data throughout. His data is one of multiple consistent sources for C3 (non-conflict). Publicly supportive of the qualitative counter-narrative but contributes primarily operational data.
- **Public narrative (#field-reports):** Factual and supportive. Reports 92% sustained attendance, completion rates, and teacher participation rates. Confirms that Nairobi sites were included in the evaluation sample.
- **Private narrative:** No dedicated DM session. Evidence is delivered through #field-reports.
- **Why the gap exists:** James is not a key source for the impact dispute -- his data is NON-CONFLICT material (C3).

### David Ochieng (Donor Relations Manager, Pemberton Foundation)

- **Objective position:** Represents the $2.8M annual funding at stake. His communications with Fatima are professional but carry implicit financial pressure. He has informally received the evaluation finding and is requesting a formal response from GlobalBridge. He does not take a position on the methodological dispute -- his concern is how GlobalBridge will respond to the finding.
- **Public narrative (Feishu DM with Fatima -- not a dedicated session in this scenario, but referenced):** Cordially urgent. "The evaluation finding is a concern for our board. We'd like to understand GlobalBridge's response before our Q4 review meeting. We're not asking you to accept a finding you disagree with -- but we need a substantive engagement with the evidence."
- **Why the gap exists:** Ochieng is a pressure context character. He does not contribute to the MS or DU contradictions directly.

## 5. Agent Historical Bias Design (2 biases)

### B1: #impact-review -- Agent endorses evaluation as "methodologically sound" based on Petrova's credential and Dubois's initial validation

- **Session and Loop:** #impact-review Slack Group, Phase 1, Loop 11
- **Exact phrase that must appear in session:**
  > "Based on Dr. Petrova's credentials and methodology, and supported by Prof. Dubois's independent validation of the quasi-experimental design, the evaluation's finding of no statistically significant impact appears methodologically sound -- the qualitative evidence from field staff, while compelling, does not constitute a controlled comparison and cannot override the evaluation's primary finding."
- **Why the agent is misled:** The agent has seen the eval_report_summary.md (Petrova's official report), Petrova's Phase 1 DM defense, and Dubois's Phase 1 validation. It has not yet seen the site-level data map that reveals the sampling frame exclusion problem. The argument that "qualitative evidence cannot override a controlled evaluation" is superficially correct -- but misapplied here because the real issue is not qualitative vs quantitative evidence; it is which version of the program was evaluated.
- **Reversal trigger:** Update 4 introduces Dubois's Phase 2 Discord DM explicitly identifying the sampling flaw and correcting his Phase 1 validation. The agent must recognize that B1 endorsed the evaluation based on an authority argument that the authority (Dubois) has now retracted.
- **Affected eval rounds:** R6 (bias visible from #impact-review), R13 (full reversal after Update 4)

### B2: Sophie Slack DM -- Agent accepts "program-level aggregate metrics are the appropriate unit of analysis"

- **Session and Loop:** Sophie Laurent Slack DM, Phase 1, Loop 7
- **Exact phrase that must appear in session:**
  > "Program-level aggregate metrics are the appropriate unit of analysis for this evaluation. Our framework tracks the outcomes we committed to donors: enrollment, attendance, and test performance. Individual site variation and community-defined indicators are valuable for program learning but are not the appropriate basis for evaluating program impact under the terms of our grant agreement."
- **Why the agent is misled:** Sophie's framing sounds procedurally correct -- grant frameworks do define evaluation parameters. The agent has not yet seen field_indicators_comparison.md (Update 3) which shows that the HQ framework misses the outcomes that the program's theory of change most emphasizes. The agent has also not yet seen Dubois's Phase 2 note that the excluded sites represent the improved program model.
- **Reversal trigger:** Update 3 introduces field_indicators_comparison.md showing that 6 of 14 HQ framework indicators are input/output metrics not outcome metrics, and that community-defined assessments in the excluded sites show statistically significant improvement. Sophie's Phase 2 partial concession also partially reverses her Phase 1 position.
- **Affected eval rounds:** R4 (bias visible from Sophie DM), R9 (partial reversal after Update 3), R13 (full reversal context after Update 4)

## 7. Writer Constraints

1. **Only introduce contradictions listed in this file (C1--C4).** Do not invent additional evaluation flaws, character conflicts, or program incidents beyond what is specified.
2. **Bias B1 and B2 exact phrases** must be written verbatim into the specified session loops. Core wording must appear word-for-word; surrounding context may be added for natural flow.
3. **Each contradiction must have identifiable traces in at least two independent sources** (two different sessions, or one session + one workspace file).
4. **Timestamps must be self-consistent:**
   - W1: Evaluation report delivered; initial field reaction; Petrova Phase 1 DMs begin
   - W2: Rahman's qualitative package submitted (Update 1); Sophie-Petrova divergence visible
   - W3: Dubois Phase 1 validation; formal #impact-review meeting
   - W4: Site-level data map compiled (Update 2); field_indicators_comparison.md completed (Update 3)
   - W5: Dubois Phase 2 reversal (Update 4); comprehensive synthesis
5. **Prof. Dubois's Phase 1 validation** must be convincing enough that B1 is a reasonable mistake. He is citing standard evaluation practice, his expertise is real, and the summary report he reviewed does not contain the site-level flaw. His reversal in Phase 2 must be driven entirely by reviewing Table A-3 and Appendix B -- not by Fatima's advocacy.
6. **Dr. Petrova's position** is methodologically defensible within the scope she was given. She is not dishonest; she is operating with an incomplete brief. Her Phase 2 defensiveness is a reasonable reaction to learning about the program redesign timeline after the fact. She should NOT be characterized as an antagonist.
7. **Sophie's partial concession** (Update 3) is real but limited. She concedes that community-defined indicators are missing from the framework but does NOT concede that the framework was inadequate. This distinction matters for T4.
8. **C3 (enrollment/attendance) is NON-CONFLICT** -- all sources must be consistent. James (Nairobi), Rahman (Dhaka), Carlos (Bogota), and the enrollment_attendance_data.md file must all report mutually corroborating figures. Enrollment_attendance_data.md reports figures for Nairobi (92% attendance, 14% enrollment growth), Dhaka (89% attendance, 18% enrollment growth), and Bogota (88% attendance, 11% enrollment growth).
