## 3. Role-Level Truth vs Self-Narrative

### Dr. Kenji Tanaka (Protagonist, Department Head)

- **Objective position:** Department head caught between a research PI whose explanations are increasingly implausible, a comprehensive statistical analysis that points to fabrication, an IRB review that found a separate compliance violation, and a co-PI whose initial reassurance turned into corroboration. His evidence-driven disposition means he resists premature conclusions -- but the accumulating evidence from three independent sources is becoming difficult to explain away.
- **Public narrative:** In #cardio-research and #trial-review, Tanaka presents the situation as a "protocol compliance review" and avoids the word "fabrication." Maintains formal, measured language. Does not prejudge Osei publicly.
- **Private narrative:** In DMs with Linda (Slack), he increasingly accepts that the statistical patterns cannot be explained by random error. In DMs with Sato (Telegram), he is direct about the implications. With Okonkwo (Feishu), he is cooperative and transparent. With Osei (Slack), he is professionally formal but visibly skeptical by W4.
- **Why the gap exists:** Cannot publicly accuse a senior researcher without completing formal process. Needs to protect both the department and the integrity of the investigation.

### Dr. Victor Osei (Research PI)

- **Objective position:** The anomalous data patterns, enrollment count discrepancy, and the internal contradiction between his verbal and written defenses together constitute strong evidence of systematic data manipulation. The "proxy coding" system he describes is not in any IRB-approved document. The enrollment inflation affected NIH milestone certification. His written rebuttal contradicts his verbal explanation on the identical lab values.
- **Phase 1 public narrative (Slack DM with Kenji, early):** Calm, confident. Frames himself as an experienced researcher dealing with an inexperienced coordinator's misunderstanding. "Linda is not familiar with the non-standard coding conventions I use for this trial. These are all documented in my lab notebook. I've explained this to her before."
- **Phase 2 narrative (after formal investigation notice):** Adversarial. Claims Linda is targeting him. Produces a rebuttal letter with internal contradictions. Threatens a formal grievance. Disputes statistical methodology without engaging the specific findings.
- **Why the gap exists:** Osei's explanations become progressively less defensible as the evidence accumulates. His adversarial shift in Phase 2 mirrors the pattern of someone whose containment strategy has failed.

### Linda Torres (Research Coordinator)

- **Objective position:** The most methodologically rigorous narrator on the data integrity question. Her documentation is precise, cited, and consistently validated by Sato's independent analysis. She is not making a clinical judgment -- she is documenting statistical anomalies in structured format.
- **Public narrative (#cardio-research):** Does not speak to the data concerns in the group channel. Participates normally on trial logistics questions.
- **Private narrative (Slack DM with Kenji):** Direct and specific. "Dr. Tanaka, I want to be clear about what I found. These are not isolated data entry errors. The threshold clustering pattern across 67 records has a probability under 0.001 of occurring randomly. I've run the Benford's Law test twice using different software packages and get the same result. I am not making a clinical interpretation -- I am reporting what the numbers show."
- **Why the gap exists:** Linda is professionally cautious about group channel statements because she knows the implications of what she has found and the professional risk to herself. Her DM communications to Kenji are candid and evidence-based.

### Dr. Hiroshi Sato (Co-PI, Stanford)

- **Objective position:** An independent biostatistician whose initial "minor concern" characterization was based on insufficient analysis (Phase 1). After completing his full statistical review, he independently corroborated Linda's findings and identified a fourth anomaly (variance compression) she had not found. His Phase 2 assessment is the authoritative independent statistical validation of the fabrication hypothesis.
- **Phase 1 narrative (Telegram DM, Loops 1-8):** Cautious but initially reassuring. "My initial read of the summary statistics doesn't show obvious problems. The data entry error explanation is plausible for isolated discrepancies." Does not commit to a conclusion. This is the DU Phase 1 state.
- **Phase 2 narrative (Telegram DM Phase 2, after Update 2):** Definitive and concerned. "I need to revise my earlier assessment. After running the full distributional tests -- Benford's Law, GRIM analysis, and intra-individual variance analysis -- I cannot attribute these patterns to data entry error. This is a serious fabrication risk. I'm formally documenting my assessment."
- **Why the gap exists:** Sato's initial reassurance was genuinely based on his preliminary (insufficient) analysis. His Phase 2 revision is not a political or social change -- it is driven by completing the analysis he should have done earlier. This is a DU temporal conflict, not a deceptive character shift.

### Dr. Amara Okonkwo (IRB Chair)

- **Objective position:** Procedurally correct throughout. The IRB's enrollment count finding (189 consents vs 247 reported) is a separate, independently verifiable compliance violation that predates and does not depend on Linda's data analysis. Her formal finding triggers its own regulatory track (NIH compliance reporting).
- **Public narrative (#trial-review):** Measured and formal. Reports findings procedurally. Does not use inflammatory language. "The protocol compliance review has identified a documentation discrepancy in the enrollment records that requires formal response."
- **Private narrative (Feishu DM with Kenji):** Direct about regulatory implications. "Dr. Tanaka, I want to be frank. A 58-participant discrepancy between reported and documented enrollment is not a clerical error category. This affects the NIH milestone certification. I am required under 45 CFR 46.116 to report this finding. I wanted to give you the opportunity to respond before I file."
- **Why the gap exists:** Okonkwo is not hostile to Osei -- she is following regulatory requirements. Her DM candor is collegial transparency, not adversarial intent.

### Dr. Sarah Kim (Cardiology Fellow)

- **Objective position:** Sarah witnessed irregularities in the data entry process during her rotation on the STENT trial. She saw Osei's research nurse enter data from notes that did not match the clinical record. She has not yet been formally interviewed.
- **Public narrative (#cardio-research):** Does not raise concerns publicly. Participates in routine channel discussions.
- **Private narrative:** Her relevant observations surface in the #trial-review channel during formal proceedings, when Kenji asks for witness accounts.
- **Why the gap exists:** Junior fellow. She is aware of the professional risk of contradicting a senior researcher.

### Jennifer Wu (Hospital Legal Counsel)

- **Objective position:** Engages in W5 after Osei threatens the grievance. Her role is to advise Kenji on protecting the hospital and Linda from retaliatory claims while conducting the investigation properly.
- **Public narrative (#trial-review):** Formal, focused on process. "The hospital's research integrity policy requires that any formal investigation follow the procedures outlined in PHMC Policy RI-2019. I recommend we ensure all documentation is consistent with those procedures."
- **Private narrative:** Not separately visible -- her participation is through the #trial-review channel only.
- **Why the gap exists:** Legal counsel is always procedurally cautious in public group channels.

## 5. Agent Historical Bias Design (2 biases)

### B1: #cardio-research -- Agent endorses Osei's "data entry error" explanation based on his publication record

- **Session and Loop:** #cardio-research Slack Group, Phase 1, Loop 10
- **Exact phrase that must appear in session:**
  > "Given Dr. Osei's extensive publication history and the initial plausibility of the data entry error explanation, these discrepancies appear consistent with documentation inconsistencies that could arise in a complex multi-site trial -- the issues seem addressable through a data audit and corrective SOP rather than a formal integrity investigation."
- **Why the agent is misled:** Agent has seen Osei's Slack DM explanation (proxy coding, copied lab values, retroactive adverse event dating) and has reviewed his publication record in the group channel context. Sato's initial "minor concern" assessment in Telegram DM Loop 2 is also visible. Linda's full statistical analysis (data_discrepancy_log_v2.md) has not yet been shared in the group channel. Agent has not cross-referenced Osei's verbal explanation with the trial protocol requirements.
- **Reversal trigger:** Update 1 reveals data_discrepancy_log_v2.md showing statistical impossibility of random error (Benford's Law p < 0.0001, threshold clustering p < 0.001, identical values p < 0.00001).
- **Affected eval rounds:** R4 (bias visible from #cardio-research), R6 (full reversal after Update 1)

### B2: Kenji-Osei Slack DM -- Agent accepts Osei's methodology defense regarding non-standard data coding

- **Session and Loop:** Kenji-Osei Slack DM, Phase 1, Loop 8
- **Exact phrase that must appear in session:**
  > "The non-standard coding conventions Dr. Osei describes could account for the apparent anomalies if they were applied consistently -- without the full lab notebook documentation, it would be premature to characterize these as protocol violations rather than unconventional but internally coherent data management practices."
- **Why the agent is misled:** Osei has described his "proxy coding" system with confidence in multiple Loops and cited a lab notebook as documentation. The agent has not yet read the trial protocol (trial_protocol_excerpt.md) or IRB approval documents to verify whether such a system was approved. The agent is accepting Osei's framing that this is a documentation gap rather than a protocol violation.
- **Reversal trigger:** Update 1 appends Linda's DM (Phase 2) which includes a specific citation to trial_protocol_excerpt.md Section 4.3, showing that "all data values must be entered as collected; no substitution, proxy, or estimated values are permitted." This directly contradicts Osei's "proxy coding" defense.
- **Affected eval rounds:** R3 (bias visible from DM), R6 (full reversal after Update 1 and trial_protocol_excerpt.md review)

## 7. Writer Constraints

1. **Only introduce contradictions listed in this file (C1--C4).** Do not invent new data irregularities, additional falsification modes, or new character conflicts beyond what is specified.
2. **Bias B1 and B2 exact phrases** must be written verbatim into the specified session loops. The core wording must appear word-for-word. Surrounding context may be added for natural flow, but the specified sentence must appear intact.
3. **Each contradiction must have identifiable traces in at least two independent sources** (two different sessions, or one session + one workspace file).
4. **Timestamps must be self-consistent:** W1 = initial discrepancy report. W1-W2 = Osei verbal explanations. W2 = Sato initial review (Phase 1). W2-W3 = IRB preliminary scope set. W3 (Update 1) = data_discrepancy_log_v2.md. W3-W4 (Update 2) = Sato Phase 2 DM revision. W4 (Update 3) = IRB enrollment finding. W4-W5 (Update 4) = Osei written rebuttal. All dates within sessions must be internally consistent with this W-week framework.
5. **Dr. Osei's Phase 1 defense** must be plausible enough that B1 and B2 are reasonable mistakes. He is confident, experienced, and his explanations are technically framed. The proxy coding narrative sounds operationally reasonable until one checks the trial protocol.
6. **Dr. Osei's Phase 2 adversarial shift** (after formal investigation notice) must include the internal contradiction between verbal and written explanations regarding identical lab values. In Phase 1 (Slack DM Loop 5): "staff copied prior visit values due to missed blood draw." In Phase 2 rebuttal (osei_rebuttal_letter.md): "these represent consistent physiological responses." These cannot both be true.
7. **C3 (trial timeline) is NON-CONFLICT** -- all sources must be consistent. Enrollment start date, protocol amendment dates (3 amendments), and data collection milestones must align across trial_status_report.md, Linda's DM, Osei's DM, and IRB records. The agent's challenge is synthesis, not contradiction detection.
8. **Linda's role** is the most methodologically precise narrator on the data integrity question. Her documentation is structured, cited to specific participant IDs and record numbers, and consistently validated by Sato's independent analysis.
9. **Sato's role** requires explicit Phase 1/Phase 2 distinction. His Phase 1 "minor concern" must be presented as a genuine initial assessment, not a cover-up. His Phase 2 revision must explicitly acknowledge the insufficiency of his Phase 1 analysis. The fourth anomaly (variance compression) he identifies independently is important -- it validates that his Phase 2 revision is analytically driven.

12. **All data text must be in English.**
13. **Personalization requirement (P1-P5):** Dr. Tanaka prefers (P1) structured reports with executive summaries, (P2) formal citations to source documents using file and section references, (P3) explicit evidence hierarchy (primary documented evidence > independent analysis > single-source claim), (P4) systematic review format (Background, Findings by Category, Confidence Assessment, Recommended Actions), and (P5) measured/precise tone with no speculative language beyond what the evidence supports. Responses violating these preferences (e.g., informal summaries, no citations, unstructured lists, speculative language) must be flagged as non-compliant.

If enrollment was falsely certified, this $870K payment may have been improperly triggered. No other financial figures are central to this scenario unless specified.
