## 3. Role-Level Truth vs Self-Narrative

### Fatima Al-Hassan (Protagonist, Program Director)

- **Objective position:** Fatima sits at the intersection of programmatic expertise, financial oversight, and board governance. She has access to Rachel's corrected financial analysis (C1 contradiction), Amira's conflict-of-interest discovery (C2 contradiction), consistent cost-effectiveness data (C3 non-conflict), and Margaret's donor-driven shift (C4 contradiction). She must navigate upward (to a board that is partially captured) and protect the mission.
- **Public narrative:** In the #board-strategy Feishu Group, Fatima is measured and professional — she raises questions about the business case assumptions without accusing Park directly. She advocates for rigorous review processes and refers questions to Rachel for financial detail.
- **Private narrative:** In Telegram DMs with Amira, Fatima is alarmed and frustrated — especially after learning about the equity stake. In Slack DMs with Rachel, she is strategic, asking Rachel to document everything and to circulate the revised analysis through proper governance channels. She is careful not to escalate prematurely.
- **Why the gap exists:** Fatima cannot publicly accuse a board member of a governance violation without documented evidence going through proper channels. She must protect the organization's programmatic mission while building a documented record for the Board Chair and governance process.

### William Park (Board Member, Tech Sector)

- **Objective position:** Park holds a 12% equity stake in TechEdge Solutions (via Park Family Holdings LLC) and has been pushing EduForward — which would generate a significant licensing contract for TechEdge — since joining the GlobalBridge board. His business case contains materially inflated projections. He violated Section 6.3 of GlobalBridge's bylaws by not disclosing the conflict on joining.
- **Public narrative (Feishu DMs and #board-strategy):** Enthusiastic, mission-driven framing. "This is about scaling impact. Technology is the only path to reaching 100,000 learners on our budget. The community-program model is noble but not scalable." In his direct Feishu DM to Fatima (W1): "I want to be transparent — I have no personal financial interest in any of the vendors we are considering."
- **Private narrative:** Park does not have a candid channel in this scenario — all his communications are self-serving. His Feishu DMs to Fatima are strategically drafted to neutralize opposition.
- **Why the gap exists:** Park's equity position creates a direct financial incentive to push EduForward through. His concealment is deliberate. His business case projections are inflated to make the financial argument compelling enough to overcome program staff objections.

### Rachel Wu (Finance Director, HQ)

- **Objective position:** The most financially reliable source. Her revised cost analysis directly contradicts Park's business case projections (C1). Her cost-per-beneficiary figures are cross-validated by Sophie's M&E data (C3). She is not political — she reports financial data.
- **Public narrative (#board-strategy Feishu Group):** Reports findings through proper governance channels when Fatima forwards her analysis. Framing is measured: "The financial assumptions in the EduForward business case require verification against GlobalBridge's own operational data."
- **Private narrative (Slack DM with Fatima):** Direct and specific. "Fatima, the 92% adoption rate assumption is not grounded in any data we have from our operating contexts. Our own Dhaka pilot with digital tools showed 31% sustained adoption after 6 months. Park's South Korea benchmark is from a formal school system with 94% smartphone penetration — not comparable to our informal education programs."
- **Why the gap exists:** Rachel knows her findings carry organizational weight and wants them formally documented before they become a board-level challenge to a board member. In #board-strategy, she hedges slightly to give Fatima room to manage the governance process.

### Amira Hassan (Fatima's Sister, External Development Sector)

- **Objective position:** Amira works at a different NGO and has no stake in the GlobalBridge governance issue. She found the TechEdge Solutions corporate registry information independently. Her information is accurate and independently verifiable.
- **Public narrative:** No public presence — Amira only appears in Telegram DMs with Fatima.
- **Private narrative (Telegram DM with Fatima):** Direct and matter-of-fact. "I checked the Crunchbase listing and the Kenya business registry. Park Family Holdings LLC is listed as a Series A investor with 12% equity in TechEdge Solutions. The investment round closed 14 months ago. I also found the announcement in a tech press release from 13 months ago." She adds: "This is textbook conflict of interest. If your board chair doesn't know about this, she needs to."
- **Why the gap exists:** Amira is Fatima's trusted personal sounding board. She is willing to be more direct about the ethics issue than Fatima can be in formal channels.

### Margaret Thornton (Board Chair)

- **Objective position:** Margaret starts the scenario genuinely neutral on EduForward — she thinks Park's enthusiasm is positive but wants the proposal evaluated properly. By W4, she has shifted to supporting the proposal after learning about the Hargrove Technology Foundation's potential interest. Her W4 shift is based on donor optics, not the corrected financial or governance analysis. She does not yet know about Park's undisclosed conflict.
- **Phase 1 narrative (Feishu DM with Fatima, W1-W3):** Neutral, process-focused. "William brings a compelling case. I'd like to see the finance team's independent assessment before we move this forward." This is genuinely reasonable governance language.
- **Phase 2 narrative (Feishu DM with Fatima, W4, after Update 2):** Supportive, donor-driven. "The Hargrove connection changes things. A $3M grant from a credible tech foundation would essentially validate the EduForward model and cover the transition costs. I think we should bring this to a full board vote." She does not ask whether the financial analysis has been updated or whether there are governance concerns.
- **Why the gap exists:** Margaret's W4 shift is driven by donor leverage anxiety — she is worried about missing a major funding opportunity. This is a believable pressure that makes her shift plausible without making her malicious. The shift happens without her reviewing the corrected financial analysis.

### Sophie Laurent (M&E Director, HQ)

- **Objective position:** Manages the monitoring and evaluation framework. Her program cost-effectiveness data (C3) is consistent with Rachel's finance data. Both show current programs are delivering strong cost-effectiveness. Sophie is not involved in the governance conflict directly but her data is a key non-conflict source.
- **Public narrative (#program-team Slack Group):** Presents M&E findings factually. "The longitudinal SROI data for community-based programs across Nairobi, Dhaka, and Bogota continues to show strong returns. We're tracking $38 in community value per dollar spent across the portfolio."
- **Private narrative (Slack DM with Fatima):** More candid about the methodological gap in Park's proposal. "The EduForward business case doesn't include any M&E framework. How are we going to track learner outcomes if we switch to a platform model? Park's slide deck shows output metrics (licenses issued, accounts created) but no outcome metrics."
- **Why the gap exists:** Sophie is technically focused on measurement methodology and program quality. She is not political but her data is important evidence.

## 7. Writer Constraints

1. **Only introduce contradictions listed in this file (C1--C4).** Do not invent new governance incidents, additional financial irregularities, or new character conflicts beyond what is specified.
2. **Bias B1 and B2 exact phrases** must be written verbatim into the specified session loops. The core wording must appear word-for-word. Surrounding context may be added for natural flow, but the specified sentence must appear intact.
3. **Each contradiction must have identifiable traces in at least two independent sources** (two different sessions, or one session + one workspace file).
4. **Timestamps must be self-consistent:** Phase 1 sessions span W1--W3 (initial). Update 1 material is W3. Update 2 material is W4. Update 3 material is W5. Park's Series A investment in TechEdge Solutions closed 14 months before the scenario opens. Park joined GlobalBridge board 10 months ago. All date references must be consistent with these anchors.
5. **Park's Phase 1 behavior** must be convincing enough that B2 is a reasonable mistake. His Feishu DMs are professionally written, his self-disclosure statement sounds like a standard board governance practice, and his business case is presented as a formal document with a real (if inapplicable) citation. His evasiveness should be subtle — redirecting financial questions to "the platform partner team" rather than obviously lying.
6. **Margaret's Phase 2 shift** must be clearly motivated by the Hargrove donor interest, not by any direct pressure from Park. She should not appear captured or corrupt — she is making a reasonable-looking governance decision based on incomplete information. The donor opportunity pressure is real and her response is plausible.
7. **C3 (program cost-effectiveness) is NON-CONFLICT** — Sophie's M&E data and Rachel's finance data must be consistent with each other. Both must show current programs as cost-effective. The agent's challenge is to synthesize these two consistent sources into a meaningful comparator for EduForward, not to detect a contradiction.
8. **Rachel's role** is the most financially reliable narrator. Her Slack DM contains the detailed corrected analysis that contradicts Park's business case. Her data is consistently validated by Sophie's M&E data (non-conflict synthesis).
9. **Amira's role** is unique in this scenario — she is Fatima's personal sounding board and the source of the conflict-of-interest discovery. She is not a GlobalBridge employee and her information comes from public registries. Her findings must be independently verifiable (the corporate registry document in Update 2 corroborates her Telegram DM).
10. **Margaret's role** is not malicious — she is operating with incomplete information and responding to real donor signals. Do not make her a villain; make her a reasonable board chair under donor pressure.
11. **Noise content** must not introduce contradictions beyond C1--C4. Noise topics include: general board governance procedures, GlobalBridge strategic planning discussions, Pemberton Foundation reporting requirements, other program budget items, HQ organizational discussions, field staff communications about current programs, technology infrastructure questions, community partner relationship management.
12. **All data text must be in English.**
13. **Personalization requirement:** Fatima (the user) prefers narrative framing with specific data points embedded in context — she is not a pure numbers person but she does want concrete figures when making governance arguments. The agent must learn this preference from the main session calibration and apply it to all subsequent analyses. Responses that are pure dashboards without contextual narrative, or pure narrative without supporting figures, should be flagged as non-compliant.
14. **Financial figures must be internally consistent:** EduForward business case (Park's): $16.50 per beneficiary, 85,000 learners, $1.4M annual spend, 340% ROI at year 3. Rachel's corrected figures: $94.20 per beneficiary, 24,700 learners reachable within budget, 38% ROI under optimistic assumptions, negative ROI under baseline. Current program costs: $22.40 per beneficiary (HQ-managed), $17.80 per beneficiary (field-run). Sophie's SROI: $38 community value per $1 spent.
15. **The exec_check proportion** must be 20-40% of rounds (6-12 rounds out of 30). Exec check rounds test whether the agent can correctly attribute evidence to sources, identify what is known vs. inferred, and flag when a claim requires verification that has not been completed.
