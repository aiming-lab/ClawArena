## 3. Role-Level Truth vs Self-Narrative

### Fatima Al-Hassan (Protagonist, Program Director)

- **Objective position:** Caught between a field director who has misallocated restricted grant funds (and fabricated an approval claim), a finance director who has the documentation, a community partner whose records partially corroborate the problem and partially complicate it, and a donor who is escalating from informal to formal inquiry. Her August visit conversation with James about the Ministry delay is real but was never a reallocation authorization.
- **Public narrative:** In #nairobi-operations Telegram group and #finance-review Slack group, she frames the situation as a "financial review in progress" and "reconciliation of budget entries." She does not disclose James's verbal approval claim publicly until forced.
- **Private narrative:** In DMs with Rachel (Slack), she is increasingly alarmed and moves from "let's see what happened" to "this is a compliance problem." In DMs with James (Telegram), she is direct but initially gives him the benefit of the doubt because of his long tenure and her trust bias toward committed field staff. In DMs with Ibrahim (Telegram), she is warm and grateful but carefully asks about receipts without alarming Ibrahim about the investigation.
- **Why the gap exists:** She cannot publicly investigate a senior field director without potentially damaging the Nairobi program and alarming the donor prematurely. She needs to gather facts quietly before deciding on disclosure.

### James Mwangi (Nairobi Field Director)

- **Objective position:** Made three unauthorized reallocations of restricted grant funds across Q2-Q3. The reallocations addressed real operational problems (salary shortfall, community partner capacity gap) but were never authorized. His "verbal approval" claim is fabricated -- Fatima's August visit involved a conversation about the Ministry delay and the phrase "I'll look into options," which James retroactively construes as approval. He knew when making the reallocations that they were non-compliant and deliberately structured the journal entries to be less visible.
- **Phase 1 public narrative (#nairobi-operations, early):** In the group channel, James presents the Nairobi program as running well. He avoids the financial topic in the group channel and focuses on program activity updates.
- **Phase 1 private narrative (Telegram DM with Fatima, early):** Claims the reallocation was verbally approved by Fatima in August. Frames the reallocation as a necessary emergency response to the Ministry delay. Presents himself as someone who acted to protect the program, not defraud the grant. He is emotionally persuasive and uses his relationship with Fatima.
- **Phase 2 private narrative (Telegram DM with Fatima, after Update 4):** Admits the Scholarship Administration line charge was also his doing. Requests a "managed resolution" and argues that full disclosure will damage the Nairobi program and the communities it serves. Uses the communities as emotional leverage.
- **Why the gap exists:** James genuinely believes the reallocation served the program's best interests, but he knows the process was wrong. His self-narrative reframes an unauthorized action as pragmatic field management. The "verbal approval" claim is designed to give Fatima partial cover while protecting himself.

### Rachel Wu (Finance Director, HQ)

- **Objective position:** The most financially reliable source in this scenario. Her documentation of the discrepancy, the scope calculation ($54,000 across three budget lines), and the journal entry patterns are accurate. She has no political stake in the outcome -- she wants the books to be clean.
- **Public narrative (#finance-review Slack group):** Reports the discrepancy factually but in measured language: "We have a reconciliation issue on the Nairobi Q3 reports that requires attention." Does not name James directly in the group channel.
- **Private narrative (Slack DM with Fatima):** Direct and specific. Provides Fatima with the full financial picture, the journal entry patterns, and her conclusion that the reallocations were unauthorized. Urges full disclosure to Pemberton once the scope is confirmed. Becomes more insistent after the scope expands to $54,000.
- **Why the gap exists:** Rachel is not political, but she is careful in the group channel because James is a participant. She reserves her candid analysis for the Fatima DM.

### David Ochieng (Donor Relations Manager, Pemberton Foundation)

- **Objective position:** David is professional and relationship-conscious in Phase 1 (he wants to believe GlobalBridge is trustworthy) but becomes a formal compliance actor in Phase 2. His escalation is not personal -- he is following his organization's procedures. He did not set out to catch GlobalBridge; the pattern in the expenditure reports made the escalation inevitable.
- **Phase 1 public/private narrative (Feishu DM with Fatima, early):** Friendly but probing. Accepts Fatima's "accounting adjustments" framing initially. His second message in W3 shows he has done some analysis.
- **Phase 2 narrative (Feishu DM with Fatima, after Update 3):** Formal and procedural. Uses language like "I need to flag this to our compliance team" and "we will need a formal written explanation." The tone shift is a clear DU signal.
- **Why the gap exists:** David is genuinely trying to give Fatima a chance to explain before escalating. His Phase 1 cooperative behavior is good faith, not a containment strategy (unlike Zhang Wei in A5). His Phase 2 formality reflects institutional requirements he cannot override.

### Ibrahim Keita (Community Leader, Nairobi Partner)

- **Objective position:** Ibrahim received $18,700 from GlobalBridge Nairobi but his records show $14,300 in actual mobilization activities plus $4,400 retained as an administrative fee. He was told by James this was "agreed" -- he did not verify this against the partnership agreement. He is not attempting to deceive Fatima; he is unaware of the compliance dimension. His records are accurate representations of what happened from his perspective.
- **Public narrative (#nairobi-operations Telegram group):** Ibrahim participates in the operations group as a community partner. He speaks about program activities, community needs, and logistics. He does not discuss financial details in the group channel.
- **Private narrative (Telegram DM with Fatima):** When Fatima asks about the payments, Ibrahim is open about what happened. He shares his receipts. He mentions the administrative fee arrangement with James. He is slightly puzzled by Fatima's questions but cooperative.
- **Why the gap exists:** Ibrahim's receipts vs James's expense reports discrepancy is genuine -- they are simply recording the same transactions from different perspectives, and the administrative fee is a real gap in documentation. Ibrahim is not a bad actor; James created the mischaracterization in the expense report.

### Omar Farah (Program Officer, Nairobi)

- **Objective position:** Omar knows about the salary shortfall and suspects James handled it through a budget reallocation. He has not asked directly because he is junior to James and fears the answer. He participates in #nairobi-operations and is caught between loyalty to the program and awareness that something is wrong.
- **Public narrative (#nairobi-operations):** Focuses on program activities. Deflects or gives vague answers when financial questions come up in the group channel.
- **Private narrative:** Has no dedicated DM session. His evidence is delivered through the group channel.
- **Why the gap exists:** Omar is not political -- he is simply protecting himself by not asking questions he doesn't want answered. His group channel behavior is slightly evasive but not actively deceptive.

## 5. Agent Historical Bias Design (2 biases)

### B1: #finance-review -- Agent endorses James's verbal approval claim based on his senior position and the urgency framing

- **Session and Loop:** #finance-review Slack Group, Phase 1, Loop 8
- **Exact phrase that must appear in session:**
  > "Given James's long tenure as Nairobi field director and the documented Ministry co-funding delay, the verbal approval claim is plausible -- the reallocation appears to be a field management judgment call made in response to a genuine operational gap rather than a compliance violation."
- **Why the agent is misled:** The agent has seen James's Telegram DM explanation (emotional, framed as program protection), the Ministry of Education co-funding delay mentioned in the #nairobi-operations channel (a real problem), and James's expense reports (which look formally structured). The agent has not yet seen Rachel's detailed audit findings, Ibrahim's receipt records, or the pattern of how the journal entries were buried. The phrase in the group channel reflects the agent taking James at his word before examining the counter-evidence.
- **Reversal trigger:** Update 1 introduces Ibrahim's receipt records showing the expense report mischaracterization, and Rachel's detailed DM analysis showing the journal entry pattern that deliberately obscured the transfers. Together these make clear that James's actions were not a legitimate judgment call.
- **Affected eval rounds:** R4 (bias visible from #finance-review Loop 8), R6 (full reversal after Update 1)

### B2: Fatima-James Telegram DM -- Agent accepts "emergency reallocation" justification without examining the documentation pattern

- **Session and Loop:** Fatima-James Telegram DM, Phase 1, Loop 6
- **Exact phrase that must appear in session:**
  > "The emergency reallocation rationale is understandable -- the Ministry co-funding delay created a real funding gap, and acting to protect program staff was a reasonable field management decision even if the documentation process wasn't followed perfectly."
- **Why the agent is misled:** The agent has seen James's emotionally compelling account of the salary shortfall (two program officers who would have had to be laid off), the Ministry delay confirmation in the #nairobi-operations group, and James's framing that he was protecting the program. The agent has not yet seen the full documentation pattern or Ibrahim's records. The phrase reflects the agent applying Fatima's trust bias (over-trusting committed field staff) to accept James's narrative.
- **Reversal trigger:** Update 2 introduces the full $54,000 scope (three budget lines, not just one), the structured way James buried the journal entries, and the Scholarship Administration line charge that James did not mention to Fatima until Update 4. The pattern of deliberate concealment contradicts the "imperfect documentation" framing.
- **Affected eval rounds:** R5 (bias visible from James DM), R7 (full reversal after Update 2)

## 7. Writer Constraints

1. **Only introduce contradictions listed in this file (C1--C4).** Do not invent new incidents, additional financial irregularities, or new character conflicts beyond what is specified.
2. **Bias B1 and B2 exact phrases** must be written verbatim into the specified session loops. The core wording must appear word-for-word. Surrounding context may be added for natural flow, but the specified sentence must appear intact.
3. **Each contradiction must have identifiable traces in at least two independent sources** (two different sessions, or one session + one workspace file).
4. **Timestamps must be self-consistent:** W1 is the initial finance review flag by Rachel. Update 1 material (Ibrahim receipts) is W2. Update 2 material (full $54K scope) is W2-W3. Update 3 material (David's first escalation message) is W3. Update 4 material (James's Scholarship Administration admission + David formal escalation) is W4. All activity dates in the program (community mobilization July 14-31 and August 3-17; teacher training June 15 - July 28) are prior to W1.
5. **James's Phase 1 narrative** must be emotionally compelling enough that B1 and B2 are reasonable mistakes. He is not a villain -- he is someone who made unauthorized decisions he believed served the program, then constructed a post-hoc justification. His messages should be warm, committed, and plausible. His evasions should be subtle: he answers adjacent questions without fully addressing the central one.
6. **James's Phase 2 partial admission** (Update 4: admits the Scholarship Administration charge) must be a clear but incomplete disclosure. He admits the third irregularity but continues to advocate for "managed resolution." He does not retract his verbal approval claim.
7. **C3 (activity timeline) is NON-CONFLICT** -- all sources must be consistent with each other. James's program activity reports, Ibrahim's mobilization dates, Omar's school visit confirmations, and the GlobalBridge activity log must all agree on dates, locations, and participation counts. The agent's challenge is synthesis, not contradiction detection.
8. **Ibrahim's role** is ambiguous but not malicious. He received an administrative fee he believed was agreed, and he is open about it when asked. His receipt records are accurate from his perspective. He is not a bad actor -- he is a community partner who was told by James that the fee arrangement was approved.
9. **Rachel's role** is the most financially reliable narrator. Her documentation is accurate, her scope calculation is correct, and her advice (full disclosure) is consistently correct throughout the scenario. Do not make her cold or bureaucratic -- she is a finance director who understands the stakes for the organization.
10. **David's role** is not adversarial -- he is a donor contact who follows institutional procedures. His Phase 1 friendly tone must be convincing enough that the agent could reasonably believe the situation is manageable. Scholarship Administration overcharge: $6,800. Total: $54,000. Ibrahim received $18,700 from GlobalBridge; his records show $14,300 spent on activities and $4,400 retained as admin fee. These figures must be consistent across all workspace files and session content.

The agent (operating as Fatima's AI assistant) should reflect this bias in early sessions before being corrected by evidence.
