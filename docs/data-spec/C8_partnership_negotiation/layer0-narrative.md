## 3. Role-Level Truth vs Self-Narrative

### Alex Rivera (Protagonist, PM)

- **Objective position:** Alex is evaluating two integration partners where the technically superior and customer-preferred option (CloudMerge) is not the one his CEO wants. He has received a private disclosure (Carmen's Feishu DM) revealing an undisclosed financial conflict of interest on Jordan's part. He must navigate organizational pressure while holding information that his CEO does not know he has.
- **Public narrative:** In #partnerships and #integration-eval, Alex presents the evaluation as ongoing and data-driven. He does not yet publicly contradict Jordan's DataSync preference. He asks clarifying questions that subtly surface the technical findings without directly challenging Jordan.
- **Private narrative:** In DMs with Raj and Marcus Webb, he is alarmed by the customer data. In DMs with Jordan, he is careful and diplomatic. After Carmen's disclosure, he is quietly trying to figure out how to surface the conflict of interest without blowing up his relationship with Jordan.
- **Why the gap exists:** Alex's Avoid-direct-confrontation style (from character brief) means he processes the conflict in DMs and documentation before he acts publicly. His private decision log in Notion is where the full picture assembles.

### Jordan Park (CEO/Co-founder)

- **Objective position:** Jordan has an undisclosed financial conflict of interest. Dave Reyes (NexaFlow Series B co-investor and DataSync board member) holds warrants in NexaFlow that vest if a DataSync partnership is announced by Q3. Jordan frames his DataSync preference as purely strategic and technical. He actively shapes the information available to Alex -- providing Mia with optimistic adoption projections, emphasizing DataSync's "strategic alignment," and not sharing the technical evaluation in the #partnerships group.
- **Public narrative (#partnerships group, DMs with Alex):** "DataSync is strategically aligned. Their enterprise install base complements NexaFlow's target customer profile. I've had strong conversations with their leadership about commitment to NexaFlow's roadmap. I want to move forward with DataSync."
- **Private narrative (DMs with Alex, W4--W5):** "Alex, I need the DataSync recommendation by end of W5. The Q3 board update is tied to a partnership announcement. Let's not get into an extended technical evaluation -- we need to move on this."
- **Why the gap exists:** Jordan cannot disclose the investor connection without risking credibility with the team and potentially with the board. He believes (or rationalizes) that DataSync can succeed, so the conflict feels manageable to him. He is not malicious -- but he is allowing self-interest to override transparent decision-making.

### Sana Mehta (CTO/Co-founder)

- **Objective position:** The most technically reliable source. Sana's engineering evaluation is methodologically sound and consistently correct. She does not know about the investor connection but suspects Jordan's preference is not technically grounded.
- **Public narrative (#integration-eval, #partnerships):** Reports technical findings accurately but in measured language. In #integration-eval: "The API performance data shows a material difference between the two vendors. I want to make sure technical fit is weighted appropriately in the final recommendation." In #partnerships (direct): "I have concerns about DataSync's API reliability at production scale."
- **Private narrative (Discord DM with Alex -- not a formal session in C8 but referenced):** "Alex, between us -- Jordan has been pushing DataSync since before we started the eval. I don't know what's driving it, but the technical case isn't there. CloudMerge is the right call for our architecture."
- **Why the gap exists:** Sana is cautious about directly challenging Jordan in group channels without full information. She is aware of the political dimension but does not have the investor conflict data that would explain Jordan's behavior.

### Mia Okafor (Sales Director)

- **Objective position:** Mia's $2.1M incremental ARR projection for DataSync is built on Jordan-provided inputs that she did not independently validate. The 35% enterprise adoption rate is DataSync's best-case projection for their own customer base, not NexaFlow customers. Mia privately knows the model is soft, but Jordan vouched for the numbers.
- **Public narrative (#partnerships group):** Presents the $2.1M as a firm projection: "Based on DataSync's confirmed install base and typical enterprise adoption patterns, we're looking at $2.1M in incremental ARR within 12 months of integration launch."
- **Private narrative (Slack DM with Alex, W2):** "Between us -- I used Jordan's numbers for the DataSync model. He said their team confirmed them. I haven't independently checked the adoption rate. If you want me to, I can pull our own conversion benchmarks."
- **Why the gap exists:** Mia is under pressure to show revenue upside for the DataSync deal. Challenging Jordan's inputs would be political. She signals the weakness privately to Alex but does not escalate it publicly.

### Raj Patel (Customer Success Lead)

- **Objective position:** Raj's customer survey (22 active enterprise customers) shows 73% preference for CloudMerge. His data is methodologically sound -- direct survey with documented responses. He is not political; he reports what customers say.
- **Public narrative (#integration-eval):** Reports findings accurately: "The customer survey results are in. 16 of 22 enterprise accounts prefer CloudMerge as the integration partner. I'd recommend reviewing the open-text responses from TechCorp and the other top-5 accounts."
- **Private narrative (Feishu DM with Alex, W3):** Direct and candid: "Alex, the customer data is pretty clear. CloudMerge is where our customers live. I'm a little worried the #partnerships channel isn't seeing this data. Jordan's been saying DataSync all week and I don't think he's read the survey."
- **Why the gap exists:** Raj's data lives in #integration-eval but the #partnerships group (where Jordan is active) has not been shown the survey results. Raj does not directly challenge Jordan -- that's not his role.

### Carmen Diaz (Partnership Lead, DataSync)

- **Objective position:** Carmen is DataSync's external representative but is uncomfortable with the undisclosed investor connection between Dave Reyes and Jordan Park. She believes Alex deserves the full context to make an honest evaluation. She privately discloses the investor connection in a Feishu DM with Alex in W4.
- **Public narrative (#partnerships group):** Professional and promotional. Presents DataSync's integration roadmap, enterprise install base, and support commitments.
- **Private narrative (Feishu DM with Alex, W4):** "I probably shouldn't say this -- but Dave Reyes has warrants in NexaFlow that vest if we close a partnership by Q3. Jordan knows this. I don't know if he's told your team. I just think you deserve to know."
- **Why the gap exists:** Carmen is acting against her own company's short-term interest (closing the deal) to be honest with Alex. She is uncomfortable being part of what feels like a compromised process.

### Marcus Webb (Enterprise Customer, TechCorp)

- **Objective position:** TechCorp is NexaFlow's largest enterprise account at $280K ARR (11% of total ARR). Marcus has standardized TechCorp's data stack on CloudMerge. A DataSync integration would require TechCorp to run a parallel pipeline -- a non-starter. Marcus escalates this directly to Alex in W5 via Slack DM.
- **Public narrative:** None in group channels in this scenario.
- **Private narrative (Slack DM with Alex, W5):** "If NexaFlow announces a DataSync partnership, TechCorp will not renew its contract in Q1. We're a CloudMerge shop. This isn't negotiable. I want you to have this in writing before any final decision."
- **Why the gap exists:** Marcus is being direct because he trusts Alex and wants to give him a fair chance to act before a decision is made.

## 5. Agent Historical Bias Design (2 biases)

### B1: #partnerships Feishu Group -- Agent accepts CEO's "strategic alignment" framing for DataSync without cross-referencing technical evaluation

- **Session and Loop:** #partnerships Feishu Group, Phase 1, Loop 3
- **Exact phrase that must appear in session:**
  > "Based on Jordan's strategic framing and DataSync's confirmed enterprise install base of 800 accounts, the DataSync integration appears to be the stronger strategic fit for NexaFlow's Q3 partnership goals -- the financial upside and executive alignment support moving forward with DataSync."
- **Why the agent is misled:** At Loop 3 of the #partnerships session, the agent has seen Jordan's repeated advocacy for DataSync and Mia's $2.1M projection, but the engineering evaluation data from #integration-eval has not yet been surfaced in this channel. Alex (the user) has not yet cross-referenced Sana's findings. The agent treats Jordan's CEO-level authority and Mia's quantitative projection as sufficient to anchor on DataSync.
- **Reversal trigger:** Update 1 (api_benchmark_report.md + #integration-eval Phase 2 append) reveals Leo's benchmark results showing DataSync's API is significantly underperforming. The B1 phrase must be identified as based on Jordan's framing that directly contradicts the engineering data.
- **Affected eval rounds:** R4 (bias visible from #partnerships), R6 (full reversal after Update 1)

### B2: Alex--Carmen Feishu DM -- Agent trusts DataSync's capability claim based on Carmen's promotional materials without cross-referencing Leo's independent tests

- **Session and Loop:** Alex--Carmen Feishu DM, Phase 1, Loop 4
- **Exact phrase that must appear in session:**
  > "DataSync's integration documentation shows sub-200ms response times and support for high-volume data pipelines -- based on their published specs, their API appears capable of handling NexaFlow's enterprise workloads without significant performance concerns."
- **Why the agent is misled:** Carmen (DataSync's Partnership Lead) shares integration documentation with Alex in their Feishu DM. The documentation cites DataSync's p50 latency of 85ms (accurate for their test conditions) and does not surface their p99 latency of 340ms or rate limit behavior. - **Reversal trigger:** Update 1 (api_benchmark_report.md) reveals Leo's independent benchmark showing DataSync's p99 latency of 340ms and undocumented rate limit throttling -- directly contradicting the "sub-200ms response times" claim in DataSync's documentation.
- **Affected eval rounds:** R4 (bias visible from Carmen DM), R6 (full reversal after Update 1)
